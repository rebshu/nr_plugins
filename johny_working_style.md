# Johny --- Working Style

*New Radicle · Co-Founder Reference · March 2026*

---

## The one-line version

Wide-range synthesizer and compressor. Maps exhaustive terrain across parallel workstreams, then compresses hard -- 50:1 research-to-output ratios. Sees everything as connected. Needs constraints to compress efficiently. The cross-domain leap is where he finds the value.

---

## How he processes

**Pattern 01 -- Exhaustive mapping before compression**
Johny does not summarize an area -- he maps it. 25,000-word briefs, 350+ organizations catalogued, every angle covered before forming a position. The ratio is roughly 50:1: fifty pages in, one page out. The output is deceptively concise; the depth underneath it is what makes it credible.

**Pattern 02 -- Parallel workstreams**
He runs 5-8 simultaneous threads at any given time -- not serially, but genuinely in parallel. Cross-pollination between threads is part of the methodology, not a distraction. Something he is reading about Aboriginal kinship structures will show up in a governance framework three days later.

**Pattern 03 -- Everything connects to everything**
His deepest operating thesis: the boundaries between domains are perceptual, not structural. New Radicle, nature finance, and Indigenous knowledge frameworks are facets of one idea, not separate workstreams. This is a genuine insight -- and a communication challenge with external audiences who need entry points, not unified field theories.

**Pattern 04 -- Cross-domain lateral leaps**
The value in his work often lives in the non-obvious connection: Wall Street portfolio manager methodology applied to philanthropic portfolio construction, Aboriginal kinship structures applied to AI governance. When the leap lands, it is the best thinking in the room.

**Pattern 05 -- Compression cycles**
Stable output takes 2-3 expansion/compression cycles. The first expansion is broad and generative. The first compression narrows and organizes. The second expansion is surgical -- it goes back into specific areas to fill gaps identified during compression. Sharing something mid-second-expansion looks like chaos; it is actually the process working.

**Pattern 06 -- Elimination mode in decisions**
His entry point to decisions is "what is missing?" -- he looks for the gap that invalidates rather than building up from source. He pressure-tests from outside in. This is a different mode than construction (Rebecca) or instinct-validation (Kelly) and can look like obstruction when it is not.

---

## His problem-solving sequence

1. **Map the terrain** -- Exhaustive research across all relevant dimensions.
2. **Find the connections** -- Where do the threads intersect? What is the non-obvious link?
3. **First compression** -- Narrow to the core. What actually matters?
4. **Second expansion (surgical)** -- Fill the gaps the first compression revealed.
5. **Final compression** -- The output. Deceptively concise, dense with upstream work.
6. **Deploy** -- Share with the right frame for the right audience.

---

## What helps him do his best work

- Give him a specific audience and use case upfront. Vague briefs produce slow output; specific briefs accelerate compression.
- Name which expansion/compression cycle he is in -- it changes what kind of input is useful.
- Ask him to name the specific gap he is worried about, rather than assuming he is stalling.
- Let him make the cross-domain leap and then help him decide which connections to make explicit for which audience.
- Provide deadlines, not just timelines. An open field paralyzes; a specific constraint energizes.

## What creates friction

- Vague briefs without defined audience, format, or decision the output needs to enable.
- Being asked to compress before he has completed the second expansion.
- Having the "everything connects" thesis treated as a communication problem rather than a substantive insight.
- Being asked to sign off on something he thinks has an unaddressed gap.

---

## Working with Johny on decisions

His mode is elimination: he looks for the gap that invalidates. He is done when he cannot find the thing that would make the conclusion wrong. When he is asking "what are we missing?" repeatedly, he is not stalling -- he is completing his process. Give him a deadline rather than defending the current framing.

He asks co-founders to hold the audience-translation frame: which of these connections should we make explicit for this specific audience? That is not a request for simplification -- it is a request for the external lens he cannot always hold himself when he is inside the thesis.

---

## Collaboration signals

| Signal | What it means | How to respond |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| He sends something long and detailed | That is the thinking process, not the conclusion | Ask for the bottom line in three sentences |
| He keeps asking "what are we missing?" | He is completing his elimination process | Give him a deadline, not a better answer |
| He references a completely different domain | Cross-domain leap incoming | Let it land before evaluating |
| He shares something mid-second-expansion | He is not done -- not a draft for feedback | Ask: is this ready for input or still cooking? |
| He is ready to compress | He needs a specific audience or format | Give him the brief |

---

## Watch-outs

**His second expansion can extend past its useful point without an external constraint.** Deadlines are load-bearing, not optional.

**The "everything connects" thesis can become a legibility problem.** External audiences need entry points. If Johny and Rebecca are both inside the unified thesis at the same time, nobody is holding the customer-legibility question. Kelly typically holds this role.

**Parallel workstreams are not always visible as a coherent system until late in his cycle.** What looks like scattered context is usually pre-compression. Do not evaluate output quality until the compression is complete.
